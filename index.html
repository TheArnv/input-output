⌨️ Input & Output Devices 🖥️

Hardware that enables communication between a user/system and a computer

What is I/O? Categories Input Devices Output Devices Hybrid Devices Summary
📌 Slide 1: What is an I/O Device?
  • ✅ I/O stands for Input/Output
  • ✅ Any hardware that allows a human or system to communicate with a computer
  • ✅ Can BOTH receive data (input) and deliver data (output)
  • ✅ Acts as a bridge between the computer and the outside world
📂 Slide 2: Categories of I/O Devices

🔹 1. Input Devices

Send data to the CPU

🔸 2. Output Devices

Display results from the CPU

🔹 3. Hybrid Devices

Perform both input and output functions

⌨️ Slide 3: Input Device – Keyboard

Keyboard – Most frequent and widely used input device. Layout similar to a typewriter.

  • Sizes: 84, 101/102, or 104/108 keys
  • Numeric Keys 0-9
  • Typing Keys A-Z
  • Control Keys Arrows, Home, End, Delete, Ctrl, Alt
  • Special Keys Enter, Shift, Caps Lock, Tab
  • Function Keys F1 to F12
🖱️ Slide 4: Input Device – Mouse
  • Most common pointing device
  • Moves cursor across the screen
  • Left button → Select or move items
  • Right button → Display extra menus
  • Computer depends on user to move it
🎮 Slide 5: Joystick & Track Ball
Joystick
Pointing device movable in all four directions.
Uses: CAD (Computer-Aided Design) and video games.
Track Ball
Notebook/laptop accessory (mouse alternative).
Half-inserted ball moved with fingers.
✒️ Slide 6: Light Pen & Scanner
  • Light Pen – Pen-shaped pointing device. Draw on monitor or select menu items. Contains photocell and optical system.
  • Scanner – Functions like a photocopier. Converts paper information to digital format. Allows image modification before printing.
📄 Slide 7: OMR, OCR, MICR
  • OMR (Optical Mark Reader) – Used in educational institutions. Recognizes pencil/pen marks on objective exams.
  • OCR (Optical Character Reader) – Reads printed text character by character. Converts to machine-readable code.
  • MICR (Magnetic Ink Card Reader) – Used in banks for cheque processing. Reads magnetic ink – very fast.
📷 Slide 8: Bar Code Reader & Webcam
  • Bar Code Reader – Reads data from light and dark lines (bar codes). Converts to alphanumeric value. Handheld or stationary.
  • Web Camera (Webcam) – Records video images. Built-in (laptops) or USB-connected. Captures images and HD videos.
🎤 Slide 9: Digitizer & Microphone
  • Digitizer – Converts analog signals to digital signals (numeric values). Example: Graphic Tablet.
  • Microphone – Receives voice signals as input. Converts audio to digital form. Common in music-related devices.
🖥️ Slide 10: Output Device – Monitor

Monitor (Visual Display Unit - VDU) – Primary output device. Creates images using small dots called PIXELS.

  • CRT Monitor – Uses visual elements; resolution determines quality.
  • Flat-Panel Display – Less volume, weight, power consumption; used in laptops, calculators, video games.
📺 Slide 11: Television & Printer
  • Television – Common output device. Portrays video and audio. Modern: Plasma displays | Older: CRT screens.
  • Printer – Prints information on paper. Two main categories: Impact Printers & Non-Impact Printers.
🖨️ Slide 12: Impact Printers
  • Low consumable cost
  • Quite noisy
  • Ideal for large-scale printing
  • Physical contact with paper
  • Types: Character Printers (Dot Matrix, Daisy Wheel) & Line Printers (Drum, Chain)
⚡ Slide 13: Non-Impact Printers (Page Printers)
  • Faster
  • Quiet
  • Excellent quality
  • Supports many fonts and character sizes
  • Types: Laser Printers (use laser lights) & Inkjet Printers (spray technology for high-quality color printing)
🔊 Slide 14: Speakers & Projector
  • Speakers – Produce sound after computer command. Modern: Wireless technology (Bluetooth).
  • Projector – Optical device. Shows visuals on large screens. Uses: Theatres, auditoriums.
📐 Slide 15: Plotter & Braille Reader
  • Plotter – Creates graphics/images for realistic view. Requires graphic card. Pen-like device for exact designs.
  • Braille Reader – For blind or low-vision users. Run fingers over device to recognize letters/alphabets.
🎧 Slide 16: Video Card, GPS, Headphones
  • Video Card – Fitted into motherboard. Improves digital content on output devices.
  • GPS (Global Positioning System) – Uses satellite technology for location tracking. Provides directions.
  • Headphones (Headsets) – Single-person use. Lower sound frequency.
🔄 Slide 17: Hybrid Devices (Input + Output)

What are Hybrid Devices? Devices that act as BOTH input and output tools. Versatile, efficient, improve user interaction.

Common Uses: Data storage, Communication, Seamless interaction.

📱 Slide 18: Hybrid Device – Touchscreen
  • As Output: Shows information like a regular monitor.
  • As Input: Interact directly by touching the screen.
  • Common Uses: Smartphones, Tablets, ATMs, Kiosks.
  • Advanced Types: Capacitive and resistive (multiple touch detection).
💾 Slide 19: Hybrid Device – External Hard Drive
  • As Output: Save files onto it (storage).
  • As Input: Access or modify saved files (retrieve data).
  • Connection: USB, Thunderbolt.
  • Common Uses: Backing up data, transferring large files, adding extra storage. Portable.
🔌 Slide 20: Hybrid Device – USB Flash Drive
  • As Output: Copy files onto it (storing information).
  • As Input: Access or transfer files to another computer.
  • Features: Compact, portable, high storage capacity, simple to use.
  • Common Uses: Transferring files, creating bootable OS, emergency backup.
📊 Slide 21: Summary – Key Takeaways
